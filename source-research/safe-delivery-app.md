# Safe Delivery App Source Research

Research target: Safe Delivery App produced by Maternity Foundation, Denmark.

Scope: public sources only. No attempt was made to bypass app login, app-store controls, APK access, paywalls, or gated partner materials.

## Summary

The Safe Delivery App is a free, offline-capable job aid, training, and microlearning tool for midwives and other maternal/newborn healthcare professionals. It was developed by Maternity Foundation with the University of Copenhagen and the University of Southern Denmark, and produced by Visikon.

The app originally focused on Basic Emergency Obstetric and Neonatal Care (BEmONC). Public Maternity Foundation material now describes a broader Safe Delivery App / Safe Delivery+ scope with 19 modules covering obstetric and neonatal emergency care plus preventive maternal healthcare.

I found public, text-extractable supporting materials such as web pages, training exercises, and a training catalogue PDF. I did not find an official open-data export, API, or complete downloadable text corpus of the app's action cards, procedure descriptions, drug lists, quizzes, or full clinical module text.

## 1. Clinical Content Scope

The public material shows two layers of scope:

- Older/core BEmONC scope: 10 modules listed in the official FAQ.
- Current broader scope: Maternity Foundation's 2025 training catalogue says the Safe Delivery App has 19 modules covering obstetric/neonatal life-saving care plus preventive care.

### Modules, Topics, and Procedures Found Publicly

| Module | Topics / clinical procedures found publicly |
|---|---|
| Infection Prevention | Hand hygiene, injection safety, antibiotic prophylaxis, respiratory infections. Maternity Foundation says the former COVID-19 module has been terminated and relevant content moved into Infection Prevention. |
| Post-Abortion Care | Removal of retained products of conception; post-abortion diagnosis/management; MVA-related skills appear in training materials. |
| Hypertension | Pre-eclampsia/eclampsia management; anticonvulsants such as magnesium sulfate. |
| Active Management of Third Stage Labour | Routine care, respectful care, placenta delivery through AMTSL. |
| Normal Labour and Birth | Monitoring labour, assisting normal birth, upright positioning, respectful care. |
| Prolonged Labour | Prolonged active phase; assisted/instrumental vaginal delivery, especially vacuum extraction. |
| Postpartum Haemorrhage | Uterotonics, oxytocin protocols, bimanual compression, aortic compression, uterine balloon tamponade, NASG. |
| Manual Removal of Placenta | Manual removal procedure; retained tissue. |
| Maternal Sepsis | Infection origins; antibiotics to prevent/treat puerperal infection. |
| Neonatal Resuscitation | Newborn assessment; bag-and-mask resuscitation; when to stop. |
| Newborn Management | APGAR scoring, essential newborn care, breastfeeding, danger signs. |
| Low Birth Weight | Feeding, neonatal jaundice, kangaroo mother care, care of low-birth-weight newborns. |
| Antenatal Care | Antenatal assessment; preventive services from conception to birth. |
| Postnatal Care | First six weeks after birth; breastfeeding counselling, danger signs, parent support for newborn care, links to mental health screening. |
| Perinatal Mental Health | Anxiety/depression awareness, screening, counselling, psychological first aid. |
| Modern Contraception | Contraceptive counselling/services; IUD and postpartum IUD training equipment appears in training catalogue. |
| Safe Abortion Care | MVA, paracervical block, medical abortion, abortion counselling. |
| Gestational Diabetes Mellitus | India-specific module found; OGTT, screening, counselling, management per national protocols; global-master integration planned. |
| Female Genital Mutilation | Public FAQ says an English animated instructive film exists and can be added to the app. |
| COVID-19 | Historical module; Maternity Foundation says it has been terminated and relevant content moved into Infection Prevention. |

## 2. Downloadable or Text-Extractable Access

Legitimate public access routes found:

- The app is free on Google Play and Apple App Store, and works offline after download.
- Official site says APK pre-installation is possible in weak-connectivity settings, but says to contact Maternity Foundation for APK information.
- The Maternity Foundation website has a public training exercises page with exercises categorized by app module.
- The Partner with us & Resources page links to a downloadable, text-extractable Training Catalogue PDF.
- The same partner page says their YouTube channel has video materials.
- The partner page says deep links can open specific content inside the app for interoperability scenarios.

No official public open-data dump, public API, or full downloadable text corpus of the app's action cards, procedure descriptions, drug lists, or quizzes was found.

## 3. Copyright and Licensing Terms

The App Store lists copyright as:

> (c) 2015 Maternity Foundation, University of Copenhagen and University of Southern Denmark

The official pages state that the app was developed by Maternity Foundation, University of Copenhagen, and University of Southern Denmark, and produced by Visikon.

No Creative Commons, open-data, or permissive reuse license was found for the clinical content. Free download does not appear to imply permission to republish, bulk extract, or integrate the content elsewhere.

The official FAQ says videos may be provided separately only through dialogue and with copyright/usage agreements.

## 4. Formal Academic or Humanitarian Use Process

There is a formal partner route, but I found no self-serve licensing portal.

Maternity Foundation says the app can be used freely without a formal agreement, including remote support. For deeper implementation, adaptation, training, M&E, implementation research, national rollout, or translation/adaptation, they work through the mHealth Competency Center.

Formal partnerships may use documents such as a Letter of Interest, Memorandum of Understanding, or bilateral/multilateral agreement. National adaptations are case-by-case and require partner resources/co-funding.
