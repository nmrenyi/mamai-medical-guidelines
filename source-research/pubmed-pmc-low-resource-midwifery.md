# PubMed and PMC Open-Access Literature for Low-Resource Midwifery Practice

Research date: 2026-04-30

## Scope

This report summarizes a targeted PubMed and PubMed Central (PMC) search for peer-reviewed literature relevant to midwifery practice in low-resource settings, with priority on:

- systematic reviews, meta-analyses, network meta-analyses, and evidence-synthesis-derived guidance;
- intrapartum care, postpartum hemorrhage, neonatal resuscitation, pre-eclampsia/eclampsia, obstructed labor, emergency obstetric care, partograph use, referral, maternal/neonatal sepsis, and related obstetric emergencies;
- sub-Saharan African, East African, Ethiopian, and broader LMIC contexts;
- records with PMID and PMCID, with PMC open-access availability as the practical ingestion target.

This is a search-and-ingestion feasibility screen, not a formal critical appraisal. Before ingestion, each article license should be checked in PMC XML or the PMC article page.

## Summary

PubMed/PMC has a useful open-access evidence base for a low-resource midwifery RAG corpus. The strongest candidates are systematic reviews on active management of the third stage of labor, postpartum hemorrhage prevention and diagnosis, pre-eclampsia/eclampsia in sub-Saharan Africa, neonatal resuscitation knowledge and Helping Babies Breathe, obstructed labor in Ethiopia, emergency obstetric referral, partograph use, sepsis, maternal near miss, and respectful maternity care.

The most productive PubMed-side filter was:

```text
pubmed pmc open access[filter]
```

Do not treat "has a PMCID" as sufficient for reuse. A PMCID means the full text is in PMC, but the legally relevant target is the PMC Open Access Subset, and article-level license terms vary.

## 2. Productive Search Terms and Filters

### Best PubMed Filter

Use this filter when looking for PubMed records that should map to reusable PMC full text:

```text
pubmed pmc open access[filter]
```

This was more precise for RAG ingestion than `free full text`, because free-to-read status does not necessarily imply permission to download, transform, embed, redistribute, or use commercially.

### PubMed Queries

Postpartum hemorrhage and third stage of labor:

```text
(postpartum hemorrhage OR postpartum haemorrhage OR third stage of labor OR third stage of labour OR active management third stage labor OR tranexamic acid OR oxytocin OR misoprostol)
AND (systematic review OR meta-analysis OR meta analyses)
AND pubmed pmc open access[filter]
```

Pre-eclampsia/eclampsia:

```text
(preeclampsia OR pre-eclampsia OR eclampsia OR low dose aspirin OR magnesium sulfate)
AND (systematic review OR meta-analysis OR meta analyses)
AND (Africa OR Ethiopia OR low-income OR low resource OR LMIC OR developing countries)
AND pubmed pmc open access[filter]
```

Neonatal resuscitation and birth asphyxia:

```text
(neonatal resuscitation OR newborn resuscitation OR birth asphyxia OR Helping Babies Breathe)
AND (systematic review OR meta-analysis OR review)
AND (low-resource OR low-income OR Africa OR East Africa OR LMIC OR developing countries)
AND pubmed pmc open access[filter]
```

Obstructed labor, cesarean access, and fistula:

```text
(obstructed labor OR obstructed labour OR obstetric fistula OR cesarean section OR caesarean section OR symphysiotomy)
AND (systematic review OR meta-analysis OR meta analyses)
AND (Africa OR Ethiopia OR low-resource OR LMIC OR developing countries)
AND pubmed pmc open access[filter]
```

Emergency obstetric care, referral, intrapartum monitoring:

```text
(emergency obstetric care OR obstetric emergency OR obstetric emergencies OR referral OR intrapartum care OR maternity care OR midwives OR partograph)
AND (systematic review OR meta-analysis OR meta-synthesis OR thematic synthesis)
AND (sub-Saharan Africa OR Africa OR Ethiopia OR Tanzania OR Kenya OR Uganda OR East Africa)
AND pubmed pmc open access[filter]
```

### PMC Queries

PMC full-text search with `open_access[filter]` is useful for retrieval confirmation, but noisier than PubMed because it searches article bodies and reference lists. Title-restricted PMC searches were cleaner:

```text
(postpartum hemorrhage[Title] OR postpartum haemorrhage[Title])
AND (systematic review[Title] OR meta-analysis[Title])
AND open_access[filter]
```

```text
(preeclampsia[Title] OR pre-eclampsia[Title] OR eclampsia[Title])
AND (systematic review[Title] OR meta-analysis[Title])
AND (Africa[Title] OR Ethiopia[Title] OR sub-Saharan[Title])
AND open_access[filter]
```

```text
(neonatal resuscitation[Title] OR newborn resuscitation[Title] OR birth asphyxia[Title])
AND (systematic review[Title] OR meta-analysis[Title])
AND open_access[filter]
```

## 3. PMC Bulk Download Feasibility

### Is PMC FTP viable?

Yes, PMC open-access bulk retrieval is a viable route for obtaining full-text XML at scale, but it should be treated as an open-access article dataset pipeline rather than generic PMC scraping.

The relevant base is:

```text
https://ftp.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pub/pmc/
```

As of 2026-04-30, NCBI is in transition:

- legacy PMC FTP files were moved under `deprecated/` on 2026-04-13;
- NCBI says legacy FTP and legacy cloud files will be removed in August 2026;
- for a durable pipeline, prefer PMC AWS Cloud Service, E-Utilities, OA Web Service API, OAI-PMH, or BioC API over a new dependency on legacy FTP paths.

### What PMC Provides

For the PMC Open Access Subset, NCBI provides:

- bulk XML and plain-text packages;
- individual article packages containing XML, PDF if available, media files, and supplementary materials;
- file lists for locating article packages;
- PMCID/PMID/DOI cross-reference data;
- automated access through approved services.

Legacy FTP bulk layout includes license/content groupings such as:

```text
deprecated/
  oa_bulk/
    oa_comm/
      xml/
      txt/
    oa_noncomm/
      xml/
      txt/
    oa_other/
      xml/
      txt/
```

### Access Terms and Copyright Constraints

Important constraints:

- Not all PMC articles are available for reuse or text mining.
- Systematic automated retrieval is allowed only through approved NCBI/PMC services.
- License terms vary at article level.
- Users are responsible for complying with article-level copyright and license terms.
- PMC Open Access Subset categories include:
  - commercial-use allowed: CC0, CC BY, CC BY-SA, CC BY-ND;
  - non-commercial only: CC BY-NC, CC BY-NC-SA, CC BY-NC-ND;
  - other/custom/no machine-readable license.

For a RAG pipeline, the safest policy is:

1. Search PubMed with `pubmed pmc open access[filter]`.
2. Resolve PMID to PMCID.
3. Retrieve PMC XML through an approved route.
4. Parse and store the article license from XML metadata.
5. Ingest only articles whose licenses match the intended deployment.
6. Keep PMID, PMCID, DOI, title, journal, URL, license, and retrieval date with every chunk.
7. Exclude protocols, retractions, corrections, expressions of concern, and custom-license articles unless manually approved.
8. Prefer XML over PDF for chunking because XML exposes article structure, abstract, sections, references, tables, figures, and license metadata.

## 4. Practical Recommendation for MAMAI

Use PMC open-access systematic reviews as a supplemental evidence corpus, not as the primary clinical authority. For direct point-of-care answers, guideline chunks from WHO, MSF, NICE, ICM, and national protocols should rank above literature-review chunks. PMC reviews are best used for:

- explaining evidence behind recommendations;
- adding epidemiology and local context;
- highlighting implementation barriers in East Africa and sub-Saharan Africa;
- supporting citations and traceability.

Recommended ingestion design:

- one manifest row per PMID/PMCID;
- XML-first retrieval;
- section-aware chunking;
- license metadata attached to every chunk;
- retrieval priority lower than official clinical guideline sources;
- source display that distinguishes "guideline" from "systematic review."
